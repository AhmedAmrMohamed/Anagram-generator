How do we know if two words are anagrams?


there are couple of easy ways to

sort and compare
we can sort the letters of the two words alphabiticly and check if they are equal
count and go
simply count the the frequency of every letter in the two words.

the first method takes O(n) while the second takes O(nlg(n)). which is ok in most cases but not in ours. because we are trying to find an anagram for a word. so if we went over every word in our dcitionary and check is they are Anagrams we end up with O(n*m).

Think like a prime

We do know that evey number can be factored to a unique set of prime numbers

We will use that into our advantage

First map every letter in the alpabet to a prime number. then go throuh your dictionary and calculate the product of every word by multiplying its letters.

you will notice that angrams have the same product

Now store the result in a hashtable where the key is the product and the value is the word itself

Now we want to find all anagrams to a word. simply caluculate it's product and get all the words of the same product
